HAIR – Healthcare Applications of Interactive Robotics is an internationally leading robotics and healthcare technologies research team based at the School of Biomedical Engineering and Imaging Sciences of King’s College London, UK. Our primary research areas are physical human-robot interaction, wearable robotics, haptics, control and design of human-centred robotic devices with applications to assistive, rehabilitation, surgical robotics and neuroscience research. HAIR team was founded in 2017 at Queen Mary University of London (UK), and it moved to King’s College London in 2024.

 

HAIR team is led by Dr Ildar Farkhatdinov, Associate Professor (UK Senior Lecturer) in Healthcare Engineering (medical robotics) at King’s College London. The team is composed of postdoctoral researchers, Ph.D. students, and part-time research assistants. Several final year Master and Bachelor students join the team annually to work on their final year projects and dissertations.

 

HAIR team is part of the Surgical Intervention Engineering research department based at St. Thomas’s campus (hospital), central London, UK. Contact details: Dr Ildar Farkhatdinov, ildar.farkhatdinov@kcl.ac.uk
